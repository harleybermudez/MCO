Dance Fundamentals

Fundamental Rhythm

In our contemporary age, there are different people who have created different dancing styles and steps that occur in reaction to musical sounds and beats. The ability to move one’s body to the beat of a song makes one a creative person.

Dancing

Those activities that are considered to be fundamental rhythm are simple natural activities such as walking, running, skipping, and sliding. Simple walking could either be done alone or it could be done in groups. Marching is also part of this fundamental rhythm because most times it is done with reactions to orders or musical instruments.

The 5 Fundamental Positions: Feet & Arms

Mastering these ten positions is the foundational vocabulary for many dance forms, providing the essential structure for movement and line.

Watch the Tutorial

Video: 5 Fundamental Positions of Arms and Feet (Proper & Mirrored Tutorial) by Sir Pat J.

Arm Positions

1st Position

Arm carriage is essential for balance, line, and expression in dance.

2nd Position

Arms open sideward at shoulder level with a graceful curve.

3rd Position

One arm is raised sideward as in 2nd position; other arm is raised upward.

4th Position

One arm is raised in front as in 1st position; other arm is raised overhead.

5th Position

Both arms are raised overhead in a graceful curve.

Feet Positions

1st Position

Heels are together, and toes are pointing outward (at approximately a 45-degree angle).

2nd Position

Open your feet sideward, pointing outward. The distance between the two feet should be about 12 inches.

3rd Position

Bring the heel of one foot to touch the arch of the other foot.

4th Position

One foot is placed in front of the other, pointing outward, with about 12 inches between the feet.

5th Position

Bring the heel of the front foot to touch the toe of the foot behind it. Both feet are still pointing outward.
